Seminar Summary
Seminar 01 | Swinburne University Ethical Hacking & Data Analytics

This seminar provided a foundational overview of the cybersecurity landscape and the critical role of ethical hackers. I learned about the five essential phases of hacking, which include reconnaissance, scanning, gaining access, maintaining access, and clearing tracks. The session also highlighted how data analytics and artificial intelligence are being utilized to predict and identify potential system vulnerabilities. Understanding these methodologies is crucial for developing proactive defense strategies in modern IT environments. This experience broadened my perspective on the intersection of security and data science.

Seminar 02 | Condeilamor Gantalao DevRoutes: Learning Roadmaps

The DevRoutes session focused on the strategic career planning required to navigate the rapidly evolving software development industry. I gained insights into identifying specific tech niches that align with personal strengths and market demand. The speaker emphasized the importance of iterative learning, suggesting the use of two-week "learning sprints" to master new skills. We also discussed how to build a professional brand through consistent project contributions and community engagement. This roadmap serves as a practical guide for my future professional growth and technical specialization.

Seminar 03 | Engr. Aisa Mijeno-Labastilla Innovative Research Horizons

This workshop explored the practical application of machine learning in academic and professional research. I participated in hands-on exercises using Python and Jupyter Notebooks to develop predictive data models. The session covered the importance of data cleaning and feature selection to ensure the accuracy of research findings. We discussed how innovative technologies can be used to solve complex real-world problems through data-driven insights. Mastering these tools has enhanced my ability to conduct rigorous technical analysis for future projects.

Seminar 04 | Accenture S2S Academy Introduction to Generative AI

This introductory course provided a technical breakdown of how Large Language Models (LLMs) and image generators function. I learned about the transformation of simple text prompts into complex outputs through neural network processing. The session highlighted the potential for AI to automate repetitive tasks and significantly boost technical productivity. We explored various use cases for generative tools in software development, including code generation and documentation. This foundational knowledge is essential for staying competitive in a tech-driven economy.

Seminar 05 | Accenture S2S Academy Responsible Use of Generative AI

This seminar addressed the critical ethical considerations surrounding the deployment of artificial intelligence. I learned about the risks of algorithmic bias and the importance of ensuring data privacy during model training. The discussion emphasized that AI should be used as a tool to augment human intelligence rather than replace personal integrity. We explored frameworks for responsible innovation, focusing on transparency and accountability in automated systems. Maintaining high ethical standards is a priority as I integrate these technologies into my workflow.

Seminar 06 | Accenture S2S Academy What is Coding?

The final seminar offered an in-depth look at the fundamental mechanics of computer programming and high-level languages. I learned about the architectural differences between compilers and interpreters and how they translate source code into machine-executable instructions. The session covered the standard phases of the software development lifecycle, from initial logic design to final deployment. We discussed the evolution of programming paradigms and their impact on modern application development. This refresher solidified my understanding of the core principles that drive all software engineering.
